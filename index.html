Community Rules
These basic rules are always in effect for everyone at all times.
  1. Be a decent human being.
  2. Respect the server. Expect good faith from all other players, since it is expected from you.
  3. Play clean. Do not use scripts, hacks, cheats and other illegitimate exploits.
  4. Know the boundaries. Do not abuse exploits or loopholes.
  5. Understand our server culture. Take the time to learn how we play this game and interact with each other. This is a complex community with a lot of history!
  6. Do not assume new players are minges. Do not witchhunt/RDM/harass new players or incite others to do so.
  7. Do not assume other players want their gameplay interrupted by staff. If they want staff intervention, they will ask for it.
  8. Do not be a piece of shit. Malice, deception, apologism, shit stirring, drama whoring, slander, gross disrespect, harassment, hate speech, racism, and other forms of degenerate behavior are strictly prohibited.
    Do not use offensive, obnoxious, or hateful language to intentionally agitate someone out-of-character.
    Do not incite or encourage toxicity or arguments in OOC chat or in our Discord server.
  9. Do not interfere with other players' ability to play the game.
    Do not cause unnecessary lag to the server or to other players.
    Do not deliberately crash the server or other players.
  10. Do not impersonate other people.
    Do not set your ingame name/avatar or Discord name/avatar to that of another player.
  11. Do not disrespect conflicts. When altercations between people escalate to moderation action, be respectful and polite.
    Do not interfere with the conflict resolution process.
    Do not disrespect the upstanding parties involved in the resolution process.
Roleplay Rules
General Rules
  1. Do not Random Deathmatch (RDM). You must have a legitimate in-character reason to kill another player.
  2. Do not violate New Life Rule (NLR). This takes effect if you are killed.
    • You are not required to forget all in-character information from before your death.
    • You can return to your place of death immediately.
    • However, you cannot re-engage with whatever you were doing that resulted in your death.
  3. Do not self-supply, unless there is no one else to fill the job you wish to buy items from.
  4. Do not metagame. Out-of-character information is never justification for in-character actions.
  5. Do not commit FailRP. We define this as the act of shutting down legitimate roleplay interaction in a cheap way.
    Examples of FailRP: killbing/teleporting/propflying away to escape combat, welding your vehicle to the world to prevent carjacking, not actually doing your DarkRP job
  6. Being AFK does not grant you immunity to anything.
    If you are afk in a public area, you fully accept all consequences of being attacked, raided, kidnapped, killed, etc. Consider going afk in the spawn protection zone instead.
  7. Do not grossly disrupt or interfere with other players' roleplay without a fun or creative reason for all players involved.
  8. Do not micspam out-of-character, and do not play ear rape through voice chat.
    Examples of acceptable micspam (within reason): hobos rioting outside government buildings, raiders taunting base defenders, excited cheering after winning a fight
    Examples of prohibited micspam: randomly playing ear-splitting noises when players are trying to talk to each other, covering up ingame noises (like footsteps, lockpicking, gunfire) with constant micspam during a raid
/event and /koswarn
In order to faciliate easier moderation of roleplay, you are required to clearly indicate certain roleplay actions that may escalate out-of-character conflicts and provoke moderator intervention.
  1. You must use the /event command to declare sensitive roleplay actions.
    Do not use /advert to declare sensitive roleplay actions. /advert is strictly in-character global chat.
    Don't metagame yourself!
    Everything you say in /advert chat is legitimate in-character information for everyone else.
    For example, if you announce you are performing a raid in /advert, the police have every right to immediately hunt you down!
    In contrast, your /event messages are only visible to moderators, which protects you from metagaming.
  2. You must declare the following sensitive roleplay actions with /event:
    • Muggings
      You must /event mug when you initiate/join the mugging
      You must /event mug over when the mugging has concluded
    • Raids
      You must /event raid when you initiate/join the raid
      You must /event raid over when the raid has concluded and you have vacated the target area
    • Kidnappings
      You must /event kidnap when you initiate/join the kidnapping
      You must /event kidnap over when the kidnapping has concluded and the hostage is released
  1. You must use the /koswarn command to warn another player before you can consider them kill-on-sight (KOS) in unexpected, ambiguous, or spontaneous situations.
    Example required situations: you mug someone on the street, you want to shoot someone who hasn't attacked you yet, someone trespasses into your base and you don't have a KOS line
    Example non-required situations: someone else mugs you, someone else is shooting at you, someone raids your base
  2. You must wait 5 seconds after sending your warning message before you can take hostile action against your target.
    If you target responds with hostility before 5 seconds have elapsed, you may engage them likewise before the full 5 seconds have elapsed.
  3. Your warning message must be clear and easy to read at a quick glance. Deceptive, confusing, irrelevant, and excessively long messages are prohibited.
    Example valid messages:
    • /koswarn dont get any closer
    • /koswarn drop your gun right now
    • /koswarn pay up or else
    • /koswarn get out of my house
    Example invalid messages:
    • /koswarn cry about it later lololol
    • /koswarn owo what's this? >w<
    • /koswarn Oh, how sad. I guess this one doesn't want to play with me...
    • /koswarn Attention, this is your final warning. You are currently operating in a restricted zone under my command, and any further unauthorized movement will be met with lethal force. Consider this your last chance to stand down and avoid engagement. Failure to comply will result in immediate tactical response and neutralization protocols being initiated.

Government
  1. You must have a legitimate in-character reason to want, warrant, arrest, kill, raid, or otherwise disturb the roleplay of other players.
  2. You must use /event to declare police raids.
    Refer to the /event and /koswarn section.
  3. Police raids have the same raiding rules as criminal raids.
    Refer to the Crimes > Raiding section.
  1. You must establish your laws within your first 20 minutes as mayor.
  2. You must place at least one lawboard in an accessible location.
  3. All of your lawboards must clearly visible and cannot be obstructed.
  4. You must /broadcast a message to notify all players once you have finished creating your initial laws.
  5. You must also /broadcast a message to notify all players whenever you make significant changes to the laws.
  1. By default, all DarkRP interactions are legal, unless stated otherwise by the lawboard.
  2. The default three laws cannot be removed, overrided, or altered.
  3. The laws must relate to in-character DarkRP interactions.
    Example valid laws: "Only pistols are legal to own", "Jaywalking is AOS", "Guns out inside PD is KOS"
  4. The laws must be reasonably clear to all players and reasonably enforceable by police.
    Example valid laws: "Hobos near PD are AOS", "Owning a gun is KOS"
    Example invalid laws: "no hobos allowed", "guns are bad"
  5. The laws cannot forbid fundamental/unavoidable player actions.
    Example invalid laws: "Crouching is illegal", "Using voice inside PD is AOS", "Standing inside city hall is KOS"
  6. The laws cannot defy the Stardust Grove RP rules.
    Example invalid laws: "Criminals can base with government", "Passive zones can be raided", "Bases can have hidden keypads"
  7. The laws cannot include gross discrimination, harassment, or hate speech.
    Example invalid laws: "All pegasi are KOS", "[player] is KOS", "[group] is [slur] and [ranting irrelevant to DarkRP]"
Criminals & Gangsters
  1. These jobs are permitted to commit classic and significant DarkRP crimes, beyond the limited petty crimes that Citizen jobs may commit.
  2. You must use /event to declare certain crimes.
    Refer to the /event and /koswarn section.
  1. You must be given a DarkRP-related, in-character reason by your client before you can accept their hit.
    Example valid reasons: "they mugged me", "he murdered my friend", "he crossed my base's kos line", "i hate the laws and the mayor wont change them"
    Example invalid reasons: "they are ugly", "i don't like them", "they said something mean to me in a Discord server"
  2. Hitmen cannot request hits from other hitmen.
  3. You cannot accept excessive repeated hits on the same target. You must use your own discretion to judge the likelihood of the target requesting moderation action.
This job demands caution. Hitmen often cause out-of-character altercations between players.
Crimes
  1. You must have a legitimate in-character reason to commit a crime against and disturb the roleplay of other players.
  2. You must use /event to declare certain crimes.
    Refer to the /event and /koswarn section.
  3. Do not excessively and repeatedly commit the same crime against the same target to the point of oppression.
  1. When mugging a player, you must give them at least 10 seconds to respond, before taking hostile action against them.
  2. You cannot demand more than $10K in a single mugging.
  1. Only criminal and government jobs can perform raids.
  2. You cannot raid player builds that have "building in progress" signs on them. Permanent public establishments (like city hall) are the exception and can always be raided at any time.
  3. You cannot start a raid while you are already inside area you want to raid. You must /event raid immediately before you enter the premises.
  4. Raids cannot engage & occupy the target area for longer than 30 minutes. You must leave the premises and /event raid over before 30 minutes have elapsed.
  5. You cannot end a raid while you are still inside the target area.
  6. After raiding a target, you must wait at least 10 minutes before you can pariticipate in another raid against that same target.
  7. You cannot participate in a raid from an excessive distance.
    For example, taking up a sniper perch across the street is permitted, but sniping all the way from the other side of the city block is disallowed.
  8. During a raid, neither side can shut down the raid by building invulnerable & impassible obstacles. Any obstacle you construct must be destructible (e.g. by molotov fire).
  1. Only criminal jobs can perform kidnappings.
  2. You cannot hold a player hostage for more than 30 minutes, if they wish to be released.
  1. Only the Hitman job can accept hits to peform assassinations.
    Refer to the Criminals & Gangsters > Hitman section for details.
  2. Placing hits on other players requires a legitimate in-character reason.
Building and Basing
  1. You cannot block access to a building with public builds. If your public build blocks access to an ownable building, you must purchase the doors to that building and claim it as your base.
  2. You cannot create "public" builds that obstruct or occupy job-specific DarkRP locations, such the police department and or the bank. Only the relevant DarkRP jobs can build in these locations.
  3. If access to any part of your public build is restricted in any way, the entire build cannot harbor any raidable goods.
  1. To claim an area as your base, you must own all doors that provide access to the area.
    For example, if you want to base in an apartment, you must own the apartment's door(s). If you want to claim the entire apartment building, you must own every door inside the building.
  2. You cannot build a base that surrounds another player's base without their permission.
  3. You cannot build flying, underwater, or mobile bases.
  4. You cannot harbor any raidable goods inside your base while you have any "building in progress" signs on your base.
  5. If you choose to protect your base with a "building in progress" sign, you must place a large, clear, and obvious copy of the sign on every entrance to the base.
  6. Any kill-on-sight (KOS) lines or signs that you place on your base must be large, clear, and obvious.
  7. Your base cannot use any props or materials with one-way visibility or one-way bullet penetration.
  8. Your base cannot be outright impossible to raid.
    9. Size Restrictions
  9. All passageways in your base that lead to raidable goods must be passable to the following playermodels:
    • The PPM2 playermodel at 130% scale (body size 1.3 in the PPM2 editor) while crouching
    • All the standard-size human playermodels (kleiner, alyx, barney, css guys, etc) while crouching
    Know the size!
    Use the models/hunter/blocks/cube075x1x075.mdl cube prop (standing upright) to quickly check the size of your passageways.
  10. All gaps that you intend to shoot through must be at least 0.5x0.5x0.5 units in size.
    Know the size!
    Use the models/hunter/blocks/cube05x05x05.mdl cube prop to quickly check the size of your shooty holes.
    11. Fading Doors
  11. All fading door props must be reachable by lockpicks and battering rams.
  12. Your fading doors must have enough space between them to fit a crouching player, per the passageway size restriction above. You cannot place fading doors back-to-back.
  13. You cannot have more than 5 fading doors in your base which block any pathway towards your raidable goods.
    Non-passable fading doors, such as storage cubbies and toggleable cover, do not count towards this limit.
  14. All fading doors in your base must be linked to an ingame keypad or button entity.
    15. Keypads
  15. You must exclusively use keypads to operate your fading doors. You cannot use your keyboard binds to operate your fading doors.
  16. You cannot have fake or misleading keypads. Keypads must always operate the fading door they are closest to and must be placed reasonably close to that door.
Passive Roleplay
Interactions with Passive Roleplayers have special rules. The Passive Roleplayer job constructs passive roleplay zones and engages in freeform roleplay, separate from everyone else on the server.
  1. Passive Roleplayers cannot engage in any DarkRP context.
    • Passive Roleplayers and passive roleplay zones cannot obstruct/intervene with normal DarkRP interactions.
    • Passive Roleplayers and passive roleplay zones cannot possess any DarkRP elements, including weapons, drugs, money, printers, and any other raidable item.
  2. Normal DarkRP jobs cannot engage Passive Roleplayers in any DarkRP context.
    • All DarkRP jobs cannot commit crimes against Passive Roleplayers or passive roleplay zones.
    • Government jobs cannot want, warrant, arrest, or raid Passive Roleplayers or passive roleplay zones.
  3. Players with a normal DarkRP job must act like a Passive Roleplayer when inside a passive roleplay zone.
  4. As a normal DarkRP job, you cannot change your job to Passive Roleplayer or enter a passive roleplay zone in order to escape from a DarkRP interaction.
  1. You are disengaged from DarkRP aspects and cannot directly interact with or influence any DarkRP elements.
    You cannot acquire items or money, carry weapons, build raidable bases, possess raidable goods, or otherwise intervene/obstruct the normal DarkRP jobs.
  2. You are the only "job" which can build passive roleplay zones, for conducting freeform roleplay with other passives.
  3. Your passive roleplay zones must be clearly and obviously marked as a passive roleplay zone at all entrances.
  4. Any base you build is considered a passive roleplay zone, whether you mark it correctly or not.
  5. You do not have immunity to DarkRP interactions. If you insert yourself into a DarkRP scenario and are injured/killed/wronged in any way, that is your fault.
PAC3/PPM2 Rules
  1. As a normal DarkRP job, your overall size cannot be smaller than 50% or larger than 130% of your playermodel's default size.
  2. As a Passive Roleplayer, your overall size cannot be smaller than 25% or larger than 300% of your playermodel's default size.
  3. Do not alter your hitboxes using PAC.
  4. Do not use PAC to place objects on players' screens or otherwise obstruct their vision.
  5. Do not use PAC to make yourself invisible, hidden underneath the ground, or otherwise imperceivable.
  6. Do not use PAC to create ear rape, seizure-inducing lights, screenshake, or other forms of sensory abuse.
  7. Do not use PAC to gain an advantage over other players in combat or roleplay scenarios, such as scouting through walls with the PAC editor's camera.
  8. You must check is explicit for all NSFW parts in your PAC outfit.
Wiremod Rules
  1. Do not create or use any of the following contraptions:
    • Any kind of teleporter.
    • Radars which track players, items, or any kind of entity through walls or across the map.
    • Vehicles that can clip through the map.
    • Devices with ear rape, seizure-inducing lights, or other forms of sensory abuse.
    • Devices which spam noises or effects simply to annoy other players.
    • Anything which crashes or cause unnecessary lag to the server or other players.
NSFW Rules

  2. You must accurately assign a Suggestive, Questionable, or Explicit rating to your NSFW sprays.
  3. All NSFW parts in your PAC outfit must have is explicit checked.
  4. You must receive consent from others players before engaging in erotic roleplay scenarios with them.
